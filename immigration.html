Business Visa

Business Visas • Global Business Mobility Visas
Overview ›

The Global Business Mobility visas are a combined category of five sponsored work routes for overseas businesses looking to establish, develop, or support UK-based operations.

The Global Business Mobility visas replace, iterate, and expand on previous UK business visas, allowing non-UK organisations to deploy certain personnel to the UK on a temporary basis. The main purpose of the scheme is to make it easier for organisations outside the UK to do business here.

Sub-categories ›
  • Senior or Specialist Worker Visa
  • Graduate Trainee Visa
  • UK Expansion Worker Visa
  • Service Supplier Visa
  • Secondment Worker Visa
Senior or Specialist Worker Visa ›

This route replaces the previous Intra-Company Transfer route closed on 11 April 2022. It is aimed at senior managers and specialist employees undertaking a temporary work assignment in the UK.

  • Overseas company must be linked by ownership/control to the UK sponsor licence holder (approved by UKVI)
  • Normally requires at least 12 months overseas employment unless a high earner (£73,900+)
  • Must score 60 points and hold a valid Certificate of Sponsorship for an eligible role
  • Salary must be at least £45,800 or the occupation going rate (whichever is higher)
  • No English language requirement
  • Maximum stay: 5 years in any 6-year period
  • Does not lead to settlement (but switching may be possible if eligible)
Graduate Trainee Visa ›

For workers transferred to the UK as part of a structured graduate training programme leading to a managerial or specialist role within the organisation.

  • Overseas and UK businesses must be linked (approved by UKVI)
  • Must have worked for the sponsor outside the UK for at least 3 months before application
  • Must have a Certificate of Sponsorship for an eligible occupation
  • Minimum salary: £24,220 per year
  • No English language requirement
  • Visa length: 12 months
UK Expansion Worker Visa ›

A UK Expansion Worker visa allows an individual to come to the UK to set up a branch of an overseas business that has not started trading in the UK yet.

  • Applicant must already work for the overseas business as a senior manager or specialist employee
  • Requires a valid Certificate of Sponsorship
  • Normally requires 12 months overseas employment unless exceptions apply (e.g., high earner £73,900+)
  • Minimum salary: £45,800 or the going rate (whichever is higher)
  • Initial stay: up to 12 months
  • Maximum stay: 2 years
Service Supplier Visa ›

Allows employees of overseas companies or self-employed professionals to come to the UK and carry out services covered by a UK international trade agreement.

  • Requires a valid Certificate of Sponsorship from a UK registered sponsor authorised by the Home Office
  • Must be an overseas employee or a self-employed professional based overseas
  • Must provide services under a contract covered by an international trade agreement
  • Must have 12 months overseas work for employer (or 12 months experience if self-employed)
  • Must work in an eligible job/occupation code and meet qualification/experience requirements
  • Stay allowed: 6 or 12 months, or CoS period + 14 days (whichever is shorter)
Secondment Worker Visa ›

The UK Secondment Worker Visa allows overseas businesses to transfer staff to work in a different company in the UK to fulfil a high-value contract.

  • High-value contract must be approved by the Home Office
  • Must have worked for overseas employer for at least 12 months outside the UK
  • Requires a Certificate of Sponsorship and an eligible occupation
  • Stay allowed: 12 months from job start date or CoS period + 14 days (whichever is shorter)
  • Extension possible by 12 months; maximum time on this route is 2 years

Skilled Worker Visa

Sponsorship Licence To Employ Workers From Outside Of The UK
What is the UK Skilled Worker Sponsorship Licence? ›

UK Skilled Worker Sponsorship Licence enables you to bring and employ workers with the necessary skills to work in the UK for licensed UK employers. Licensed UK businesses can issue Certificates of Sponsorship (CoS) to eligible non-UK nationals, whether located within the UK or outside the UK, enabling them to apply for a work visa (e.g. Skilled Worker Visa).

From April 2024, the requirement to renew the sponsor licence every four years has been removed. Licences with an expiry date on or after 6 April 2024 are automatically extended for an additional 10 years.

Sponsor licence requirements ›
  • Be genuine, operating, and trading lawfully in the UK
  • Be UK based
  • Not represent a threat to immigration control
  • Have key personnel who are “honest and dependable”
  • Have an adequate HR system in place to carry out sponsor obligations efficiently
Business eligibility requirements ›

Businesses applying for a sponsor licence must be genuine and legally operating in the UK as a PLC, Ltd, LLP or Sole Trader. A start up business may apply.

Businesses trading for less than 18 months must provide evidence of a current corporate bank account with an FCA and PRA-registered UK bank.

Suitability requirements ›
  • Have suitable HR systems in place to meet sponsor duties
  • Be ready for UKVI compliance visits (announced or unannounced)
  • Have no unspent criminal convictions for previous immigration offences or tax evasion
  • Have no evidence of non-compliance with previous sponsorship licences
Genuine business need ›

Businesses must show a genuine need to hire overseas workers. They must intend to sponsor workers for eligible roles and meet the skill and salary requirements for each job type. The Home Office checks the roles make sense for the business and its existing jobs. This test applies at application stage and throughout the licence period.

Key personnel ›
  • Authorising officer: senior competent person responsible for actions of staff and SMS
  • Key contact: main contact with UKVI
  • Level 1 and Level 2 users: day-to-day management of the licence
Required documents (Appendix A) ›

Required documents are explained in Appendix A. In most cases, you will be asked to submit a minimum of 4 documents. Depending on your business and the type of licence, UKVI may request additional evidence.

  • Accounts for the most recent financial year
  • Contracts for goods/services covering at least 12 months before application
  • Evidence of a UK corporate bank account (FCA/PRA-registered)
  • VAT registration certificate
  • Evidence of registration
  • Endorsement from a governing body (if applicable)
  • Proof of ownership or lease of premises
Additional supporting documents (examples) ›
  • Organisation hierarchy chart
  • Employer’s liability insurance certificate (£5m minimum)
  • Self-assessment tax return to HMRC (SA300 or SA302)
  • Company Tax Return (CT620 or CT600)
  • Premises alcohol licence (if relevant)

Please note: this list is not exhaustive.

Fees ›

Small or charitable sponsor fee: £536.00

To be classed as small/charitable, meet at least two:

  • Annual turnover £10.2m or less
  • Total assets £5.1m or less
  • 50 employees or less

Medium or large sponsor fee: £1,476.00

UK Global Talent Visa

An unsponsored work route for leaders and potential leaders
Overview ›

The UK Global Talent visa is an unsponsored work route for professionals working in certain specialist fields. It provides flexibility compared to other UK work visas and does not require sponsorship by a licensed employer.

You can apply if you are a leader or potential leader in one of the following fields:

  • Academia or research
  • Arts and culture
  • Digital technology

The Global Talent visa can also lead to fast-tracked settlement. Exceptional Talent applicants may qualify for settlement after 3 years, and Exceptional Promise applicants after 5 years.

Core Requirements ›
  • You must be aged 18 or over
  • You must have an endorsement from an approved endorsing body OR hold an eligible prestigious prize
  • Your application must not fall under general grounds for refusal
  • You must provide a valid TB certificate if required
Endorsement Process ›

Most applicants must first obtain an endorsement confirming exceptional talent or promise. Endorsements are available in:

  • Science, engineering, medicine
  • Social sciences and humanities
  • Digital technology
  • Arts and culture (including film, TV, fashion, architecture)

Each endorsing body sets its own criteria. Applicants usually need a strong body of work and expert references.

Prestigious Prize Route ›

If you have been awarded a qualifying prestigious prize listed in Appendix Global Talent: Prestigious Prizes, you can skip the endorsement stage and apply directly for the visa.

Talent vs Promise Criteria ›
  • Exceptional Talent: proven leaders with significant contributions in their field
  • Exceptional Promise: early-career professionals showing strong future leadership potential
English & Maintenance Requirements ›
  • No English language requirement
  • No maintenance funds requirement
Visa Length ›

Global Talent visas are granted for up to 5 years at a time and can be extended.

Switching Inside the UK ›

You may be able to switch into the Global Talent route from inside the UK.

You cannot switch if you are currently in the UK as:

  • Visitor
  • Short-term student
  • Parent of a Child Student
  • Seasonal worker
  • Domestic worker in a private household
  • On immigration bail
  • Outside the rules permission (e.g. compassionate grounds)
Work Rights ›
  • Full right to work
  • Self-employment permitted
  • Voluntary work permitted
  • Cannot work as a professional sportsperson or coach
  • Study permitted
  • No access to public funds
Family Members ›

You can bring dependants:

  • Spouse or civil partner
  • Unmarried partner
  • Children under 18 at first application

UK Self-Sponsorship Visa

UK Self-Sponsorship Visa as a Skilled Worker
Overview ›

One of the most common ways to work and live in the UK as a migrant is through sponsorship as a Skilled Worker. In this case, a migrant’s leave in the UK is tied to their work for their sponsored employer.

If you have already set up a company or are planning to set up a company, it may be possible to sponsor yourself in the UK. Self-sponsorship typically involves a three-stage process.

The 3-Stage Process ›
  • Stage 1: Sponsor Licence
  • Stage 2: Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)
  • Stage 3: Skilled Worker Visa Application
Stage 1 – Sponsor Licence ›

The first stage is to establish a business and apply for a Skilled Worker Sponsorship Licence. To obtain a sponsorship licence, the company must be a genuine business operating lawfully in the UK.

When applying, the company must provide at least four documents from the Home Office list (this list is extensive and not limited to the examples below). Further specified evidence may also be required.

Stage 1 – Example Supporting Documents ›
  • Employer’s liability insurance covering at least £5 million from an insurer authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)
  • Proof of registration with HMRC
  • Latest acknowledgement of a Company Tax Return
  • HMRC VAT registration certificate
  • Most recent annual accounts
  • Proof of ownership or lease of business premises
  • Latest corporate/business bank statement from a bank regulated by the FCA or PRA

The Home Office may request additional documents depending on the business and the route.

Key Roles & Compliance Duties ›

Holding a sponsorship licence carries ongoing duties and responsibilities. A sponsor licence can be suspended, revoked, or downgraded if the sponsor does not comply with the rules.

Key personnel roles typically include:

  • Authorising Officer: senior competent person responsible for staff actions and the Sponsor Management System (SMS)
  • Key Contact: main point of contact with the Home Office
  • Level 1 User: responsible for day-to-day management of the SMS

These roles may be filled by the same person or multiple people. However, the Level 1 User must be an employee or office holder. The Level 1 User must be a British or settled person, and cannot be a relative or partner of the proposed migrant worker.

Stage 2 – Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) ›

Once the company has obtained a sponsor licence, it must apply for and assign a Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS). The CoS is the formal confirmation of the employment details.

If the proposed worker is in the UK, the CoS may need to be assigned as an undefined CoS.

When assigning the CoS, the sponsor must select an occupation code that accurately reflects the role, and the job description must match the duties the proposed worker will undertake.

Stage 3 – Skilled Worker Visa Application ›

Once the CoS is assigned, the proposed worker can apply for a Skilled Worker visa.

Key requirements can include:

  • Aged 18 or over
  • Hold a valid CoS
  • Genuine vacancy and appropriate skill level
  • Sponsor has paid any Immigration Skills Charge (where required)
  • Meet English language requirement (at least CEFR Level B1)
  • Salary meets or exceeds the general threshold and/or the going rate for the occupation code
  • Meet the maintenance requirement (unless exempt)
FAQs ›

Does this route lead to settlement?
Yes. Applicants may be eligible to apply for settlement after 5 years spent on a Skilled Worker visa (subject to requirements).

Does the worker need to take a salary?
Yes. Skilled Workers must be paid a salary that meets the relevant Skilled Worker salary requirements.

Can I open a company while I am in the UK?
This depends on your immigration status and conditions. It may be possible to set up a business while you are in the UK.

Can I do other work outside my company?
Skilled Workers may be able to take additional work, but usually only if it is in the same profession and at the same level as the role on the CoS, or if it is on an eligible shortage/approved list. Additional work should not exceed 20 hours per week and must be outside main sponsored working hours.

UK Graduate Visa

Stay in the UK for at least 2 years after completing your course
Overview ›

A Graduate visa permits foreign national students to stay in the UK for at least 2 years after successfully completing their course in the UK. At the time of the application, the applicant must be in the UK.

This route is a temporary and unsponsored work route. Applicants do not need a Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) and do not need a job offer meeting minimum skill and salary levels.

What you can do on a Graduate Visa ›

The Graduate visa can be used to work in the UK or to remain in the UK while looking for work. If granted, you can earn an income and gain work experience across a wide range of UK businesses during your visa period.

How long is it valid for? ›
  • Usually 2 years if you completed an eligible bachelor’s or master’s degree
  • Usually 3 years if you completed a PhD or other doctoral qualification
Graduate Visa Requirements ›

The Graduate route is a flexible work route for overseas students already in the UK who want to find employment. Under this route, applicants do not need to prove a job offer.

Applicants are also not required to show funds or demonstrate English language ability, as these requirements will have been satisfied under the Student Visa route. However, the eligibility requirements below must still be met.

Successful Completion Requirement ›

The applicant must have been sponsored by a Student sponsor in the UK and must have successfully completed the course undertaken during their last grant of leave on the Student route.

The Student sponsor must have informed the Home Office that the applicant has completed the course by the time the Graduate visa application is made.

Qualification Requirement ›

The applicant must have completed a course of study for which they have been, or are due to be, awarded:

  • a UK bachelor’s degree
  • a UK postgraduate degree (master’s)
  • a PhD or other doctoral qualification
  • or one of the prescribed professional qualifications at degree level or above
Studying in the UK Requirement ›

Under the “study in the UK” requirement, the applicant must have held permission as a Student (granted to study the relevant qualification in the UK) for a minimum period. The required period can depend on the course length and the applicant’s study history.

Dependants ›

Following a government announcement in December 2023, Graduate visa holders will no longer be permitted to bring family members with them to the UK as dependants.

Innovator Founder Visa

For entrepreneurs establishing an innovative business in the UK
Overview ›

The Innovator Founder visa is for entrepreneurs and experienced businesspeople wishing to establish a business in the United Kingdom.

Applicants do not need to have a specific level of funds to invest. However, the proposed business must be innovative, viable, and scalable, supported by a business plan approved by an endorsing body.

Innovator Founder visas are typically granted for 3 years and can lead to settlement (Indefinite Leave to Remain) if you meet the relevant requirements.

Eligibility ›

Your business idea must be:

  • New: you cannot join a business that is already trading
  • Innovative: you must have an original idea that is different from anything else on the market
  • Viable: the business must be capable of growth
  • Scalable: you must show planning for job creation and growth into national/international markets
Knowledge of English ›

You must prove you can read, write, speak, and understand English at B2 CEFR Level.

Extension ›

You can extend your Innovator Founder visa at the end of the 3 years, or if you are eligible, you can apply for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR).

You must provide an endorsement letter from the endorsing body at each application stage.

Dependants ›

Innovator Founders can bring a partner and children under 18 to the UK.

Extension Visas

Applications for Further Leave to Remain
Overview ›

We assist with applications for further leave to remain (visa extensions) across a range of UK immigration categories.

Extension applications must meet specific route requirements and are time-sensitive. Proper preparation of documents and compliance evidence is essential to avoid refusals.

Categories We Cover ›
  • Skilled Worker Visa extensions
  • Family and Spouse Visa extensions
  • Global Business Mobility Visa extensions
  • Innovator Founder Visa extensions
  • Global Talent Visa extensions
  • ECAA Turkish Businessperson Visa extensions
What We Check Before Applying ›
  • Ongoing eligibility under your current visa route
  • Compliance with sponsorship or endorsement conditions (where required)
  • Salary and role requirements (for sponsored routes)
  • Relationship and cohabitation evidence (for family routes)
  • Business progress and endorsement status (for entrepreneur routes)
  • Absence and residence history where relevant
Timing & Strategy ›

Extension applications should normally be submitted before your current permission expires. Early review helps identify risks, document gaps, and opportunities to move toward settlement (ILR) where available.

UK Visitor Visa

Short Stay Visits to the United Kingdom
Overview ›

If you plan to visit the UK for tourism, sightseeing, or to see family and friends, and you are from outside the European Economic Area (EEA) or Switzerland, you will normally need to apply for a UK Standard Visitor Visa.

Applications must be made from outside the UK and assessed under the visitor rules. You must show that you are a genuine visitor and will leave the UK at the end of your stay. Failure to meet the requirements can result in refusal and loss of the application fee.

Visa Length ›
  • Standard visitor visas are usually granted for up to 6 months
  • Up to 12 months may be possible for private medical treatment or academic research
  • Long-term visitor visas are available for 2, 5, or 10 years
  • Even with a long-term visa, each stay is normally limited to 6 months per visit
  • Biometric residence permit may be required for longer permissions
Permitted Activities ›
  • Tourism and holidays
  • Visiting family and friends
  • Volunteering up to 30 days with a registered charity
  • Transit through the UK
  • Business visits such as meetings and interviews
  • Permitted paid engagements as an expert (lectures, performances, etc.)
  • School exchange programmes
  • Short recreational courses (up to 30 days)
  • Study, placements, or exams
  • Academic, senior doctor, or dentist visits
  • Private medical treatment
Not Allowed on Visitor Visa ›
  • No paid or unpaid work for a UK company (except permitted paid engagements)
  • No self-employment
  • No access to public funds or benefits
  • Cannot live in the UK through frequent or successive visits
  • Cannot marry or register a civil partnership (requires Marriage Visitor Visa)
Supporting Documents ›

Documents depend on your circumstances, but typically include:

  • Valid passport (blank visa page required)
  • Travel dates and itinerary with accommodation details
  • Travel history for the past 10 years
  • Employment and employer details
  • Details of people you will visit
  • Proof of relationship (if visiting someone)
  • Criminal conviction details (if any)
  • Evidence of ties to home country (job, studies, family)
  • Proof of sufficient funds for your stay
  • Certified translations for non-English/Welsh documents

After submitting the form, you must attend a visa application centre to provide fingerprints and a photograph.

Processing Time & Funds ›

Standard visitor visa applications from overseas are usually processed in about 3 weeks, subject to Home Office service standards.

You must show sufficient funds to cover travel, accommodation, and living costs for yourself and any dependants during your visit.

Settlement and Naturalisation

Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) and British Citizenship
Overview ›

Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) is a form of settlement that allows non-EU citizens to live, work, and study in the UK without immigration restrictions. In many cases, ILR is also the first step towards applying for British citizenship (naturalisation).

The qualifying residence period depends on the immigration route. Most applicants qualify after 5 years, but some routes may allow a faster pathway (for example, Innovator Founder Visa or Global Talent Visa may lead to settlement after 3 years, depending on eligibility).

Dependants ›

You may be able to apply for settlement as a dependant after 5 years in the UK, depending on your route. Applications can be made for:

  • Dependent partner (husband, wife, civil partner, or unmarried partner)
  • Dependent children under the age of 18
General ILR Requirements ›

The exact requirements vary depending on the visa category and personal circumstances, but the general requirements commonly include:

  • Lawful residence in the UK for the required qualifying period
  • No breach of immigration laws and (usually) no serious criminal record
  • Absences normally not exceeding 180 days in any 12-month period during the qualifying period
  • Passing the Life in the UK test
  • Meeting the English language requirement (typically B1 CEFR), where applicable
ILR Supporting Documents ›

Documents depend on the route, but often include:

  • Current valid passport (or travel document if you do not have a passport)
  • Birth certificate(s)
  • Any Biometric Residence Permits (BRPs) you have been issued
  • Evidence of continuous residence and absences from the UK (if applicable)
  • Evidence of cohabitation (partner routes)
  • Evidence of accommodation
  • Police certificate (if applicable)
  • Evidence/records of permitted benefits (if applicable)
  • B1 English language test certificate (Home Office-approved provider), where required
  • Life in the UK test pass notification
  • Any documents supporting changes in circumstances (if applicable)
FAQs (ILR) ›

I have ILR, and my child was born in the UK. What is my child’s status?

If a child is born in the UK while a parent holds ILR, the child will generally be British automatically. If you obtained ILR after the child’s birth, you may need to register your child as British.

Will prior criminal convictions affect my ILR application?

Previous convictions can affect an application depending on the type of offence and the timing. In some cases, it may be more appropriate to apply for an extension before applying for settlement.

British Citizenship (Naturalisation) ›

While ILR allows you to live in the UK permanently, it is not the same as British citizenship. ILR does not provide voting rights, and it can be lost if you leave the UK for a long period or if you become liable for deportation following criminality.

British citizenship provides the right to live and work in the UK without immigration restrictions and enables you to apply for a British passport.

Naturalisation Requirements ›

Naturalisation requirements depend on your circumstances, but commonly include:

  • Age 18 or over
  • Holding ILR (or settled status) for at least 12 months at the date of application (in most cases)
  • Meeting the “good character” requirement
  • Usually 5 years’ residence in the UK (some applicants may qualify under different rules)
  • No excessive absences during the qualifying period, including limited absences in the last 12 months
  • Meeting English language and Life in the UK requirements
  • Being physically present in the UK on the first day of the qualifying period (usually 5 or 3 years before the application date)
Referees for Naturalisation ›

You must provide details of two referees who have known you personally for at least 3 years.

  • One referee must have professional standing (e.g., accountant, police officer, chemist, etc.)
  • The second referee must be a British citizen holding a British passport

Referees must complete declarations and provide their personal details for your online application.

FAQs (Naturalisation) ›

Can I travel after submitting a naturalisation application?

In many cases, you can keep your passport if you have included a copy in the application and you have valid permission to travel in and out of the UK while the application is being decided.

Family Visa

Partner & Family Visas (Spouse Visa)
Overview ›

The UK Spouse Visa is for married partners of British or Irish citizens, persons who are settled in the UK, persons with pre-settled status, persons with a Turkish Businessperson or Turkish Worker visa, and persons with refugee status or humanitarian protection who wish to join, or stay with, their husband or wife in the UK.

The UK Spouse Visa can lead to settlement (Indefinite Leave to Remain) after 5 years on the partner route, subject to eligibility.

Key Requirements (Summary) ›

To qualify for a UK Spouse Visa, you must satisfy UK Visas & Immigration that:

  • Your partner is British or Irish, settled in the UK, has pre-settled status, holds a Turkish Businessperson/Turkish Worker visa, or has refugee status/humanitarian protection
  • You and your partner are both aged 18 or over
  • You and your partner have met in person
  • You are not within a prohibited degree of relationship
  • You are legally married in a marriage recognised in the UK
  • Your relationship is genuine and subsisting
  • You intend to live together permanently in the UK
  • Any previous relationship has broken down permanently
  • You meet the financial requirement
  • You have adequate accommodation
  • You meet the English language requirement (unless exempt)

Requirements can vary depending on your circumstances. For tailored advice, please contact our firm.

Relationship Requirement ›

The relationship must meet the following requirements:

  • You and your partner must not be within a prohibited degree of relationship
  • You and your partner must have met in person
  • You must be legally married
  • Your relationship must be genuine and subsisting
  • You must intend to live together permanently in the UK
  • Any previous relationship must have broken down permanently
Requirement to Have Met in Person ›

You and your spouse must have met in person. “Met” means you must be able to demonstrate a face-to-face meeting.

Marriage Recognised in the UK ›

To qualify, you and your partner must be legally married in a marriage that is recognised in the UK. You will normally need documentary evidence of the marriage.

Marriage in the UK ›

To be recognised as valid, marriages in the UK must be monogamous and must comply with relevant UK marriage law (depending on the UK jurisdiction). A marriage in the UK must be evidenced by a marriage certificate.

Marriage Outside the UK ›

An overseas marriage may be recognised in the UK if:

  • The type of marriage is recognised in the country where it took place
  • The marriage was properly conducted under the law of that country
  • There is nothing in the laws of either person’s country of domicile that prevents recognition
  • Any previous marriages of the couple have broken down permanently

Marriage outside the UK must be evidenced by an appropriate equivalent to a marriage certificate, valid under the law in the relevant country.

Genuine & Subsisting Relationship ›

You must provide evidence that your relationship is genuine and subsisting. Decisions are made on a case-by-case basis based on all the evidence available.

Factors that may be considered include:

  • You are in a long-term relationship
  • You live together as a couple
  • You have children together and share parental responsibility
  • You share financial responsibilities
  • You intend to live in the UK permanently
Financial Requirement ›

To show you can maintain yourselves in the UK without recourse to public funds, you must meet the spouse visa financial requirement (unless exempt).

Previously, the minimum income requirement was:

  • £18,600 per year, plus
  • £3,800 for a first child, plus
  • £2,400 for each additional child

Different rules may apply where the sponsor receives certain state benefits, in which case the test is typically whether the sponsor can “adequately maintain and accommodate” the applicant.

Financial requirements must generally be met when you first apply, when you extend, and when you apply for ILR as a spouse.

Note: From 11 April 2024, the minimum income requirement for the five-year partner route is stated to rise from £18,600 to £29,000, and the marginal income requirement for child dependants will be removed.

Accommodation Requirement ›

You must show there will be adequate accommodation available for you and your partner (and any dependants), without relying on public funds.

You should provide evidence that you are legally and exclusively entitled to occupy the property and that it will not be overcrowded or breach public health regulations.

English Language Requirement ›

When applying for entry clearance or switching into the spouse/partner route, you normally need English to at least CEFR A1. When applying for further leave to remain as a spouse, you normally need CEFR A2.

You may meet the requirement by:

  • Being a national of a majority English-speaking country
  • Passing an approved English language test with an app
