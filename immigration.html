Business Visa

Global Business Mobility Routes

The Global Business Mobility visas are a combined category of five sponsored work routes for overseas businesses looking to establish, develop, or support UK-based operations. These routes replace and expand previous intra-company and business transfer visas and are designed to make it easier for non-UK organisations to operate and grow in the UK market.

Under this framework, eligible overseas businesses can deploy key personnel to the UK on a temporary basis through approved sponsor arrangements.

Global Business Mobility sub-categories

  • Senior or Specialist Worker Visa
  • Graduate Trainee Visa
  • UK Expansion Worker Visa
  • Service Supplier Visa
  • Secondment Worker Visa

Senior or Specialist Worker Visa

This route replaced the former Intra-Company Transfer route. It is designed for senior managers and specialist employees assigned to a UK business linked to their overseas employer.

  • UK sponsor must hold an approved Sponsor Licence
  • Overseas and UK entities must be linked by ownership or control
  • Normally 12 months prior overseas employment required (unless high earner £73,900+)
  • Minimum salary: £45,800 or occupation going rate (whichever is higher)
  • No English language requirement
  • Does not directly lead to settlement (but switching may be possible)
  • Maximum stay: 5 years in any 6-year period

Graduate Trainee Visa

For workers transferred to the UK as part of a structured graduate training programme leading to managerial or specialist roles.

  • Must be employed by linked overseas business
  • Minimum 3 months overseas employment before application
  • Role must be on eligible occupation list
  • Minimum salary: £24,220 per year
  • No English language requirement
  • Visa length: up to 12 months

UK Expansion Worker Visa

Allows overseas businesses to send senior managers or specialists to the UK to establish a branch or subsidiary that has not yet started trading.

  • For new UK market entry by overseas company
  • Applicant must already work for overseas business
  • Normally 12 months prior employment required (exceptions for high earners / Japanese route)
  • Minimum salary: £45,800 or going rate
  • Initial stay: up to 12 months
  • Maximum stay: 2 years

Service Supplier Visa

For overseas employees or self-employed professionals delivering services in the UK under an eligible international trade agreement.

  • Requires UK sponsor authorised for this route
  • Must be overseas employee or self-employed provider
  • 12 months overseas work experience (or professional experience if self-employed)
  • Must meet occupation code and qualification requirements
  • Stay length: 6–12 months depending on agreement

Secondment Worker Visa

Allows overseas businesses to second staff to a UK company to fulfil an approved high-value contract.

  • High-value contract must be Home Office approved
  • Minimum 12 months overseas employment required
  • UK role must be on eligible occupation list
  • Initial stay: up to 12 months
  • Extension possible up to maximum 2 years
