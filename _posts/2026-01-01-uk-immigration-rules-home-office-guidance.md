The UK immigration system continues to change, with applicants expected to follow the latest Immigration Rules, Home Office guidance and digital status requirements before making an application.

In 2026, one of the most important points for applicants is that the Immigration Rules are not fixed. GOV.UK regularly publishes Statements of Changes to the Immigration Rules, which means applicants should always check the most recent rules before applying, rather than relying on old advice or previous application requirements.

## What are the UK Immigration Rules?

The Immigration Rules set out the legal requirements for entering, staying, working, studying, joining family, settling or becoming a British citizen in the UK.

Applicants may need to meet requirements relating to:

- identity and nationality
- financial evidence
- English language
- sponsorship
- relationship evidence
- lawful residence
- previous immigration history
- suitability and refusal grounds

## Why Home Office Guidance Matters

The Immigration Rules explain the legal framework, but Home Office guidance explains how applications are considered in practice.
