Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16/02/2026

Benchmark Legals (“we”, “us”, “our”) is committed to protecting your privacy and handling personal data responsibly and transparently. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store and protect your personal data when you visit our website or contact us for immigration advisory services.

We process personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Who We Are

Benchmark Legals is a UK immigration advisory practice.

2. Personal Data We Collect

  • Name and surname
  • Email address and phone number
  • Postal address
  • Date of birth and nationality (where relevant)
  • Immigration and case information you provide
  • Identity and verification documents you submit
  • Communications and correspondence
  • Technical website usage data (such as IP address, device type, pages visited)

3. How We Obtain Your Data

  • When you submit a contact or enquiry form
  • When you email or call us
  • When you request a consultation
  • When you instruct us to act for you
  • From authorised third parties connected to your case (where permitted)
  • From public authorities where permitted by law

4. How We Use Your Data

  • To respond to enquiries and provide information
  • To assess your immigration matter and available options
  • To provide advisory services and prepare applications
  • To verify identity and prevent fraud
  • To communicate with you about your matter
  • To maintain records and meet legal/regulatory obligations
  • To improve our website and service quality
Important: Submitting an enquiry does not automatically create an adviser-client relationship unless formally agreed in writing. Please avoid sending highly sensitive original documents until secure communication methods are confirmed.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data under one or more lawful bases, depending on the circumstances:

  • Steps prior to entering a contract
  • Performance of a contract
  • Legal obligation
  • Legitimate interests
  • Consent (where required)

Immigration matters may involve sensitive personal data. We process such data only where legally permitted and where necessary for legal claims and regulatory purposes.

6. Sharing Your Data

We share personal data only where necessary and lawful, including:

  • With UK government departments and authorities (for applications and representations)
  • With courts and tribunals where required
  • With regulators where required
  • With professional advisers (where necessary)
  • With secure service providers supporting our operations

We do not sell personal data.

7. Regulatory Verification Technology

Our website may display regulatory verification tools (such as the IAA/Yoshki digital badge). When loaded, limited technical information may be processed to support real-time verification of regulatory status.

8. International Data Transfers

Some technology providers may process data outside the UK. Where this occurs, appropriate safeguards are applied as required by UK data protection law.

9. Data Retention

  • General enquiries — up to 24 months
  • Client case files — typically 6 years or as required by regulation
  • Compliance records — as legally required

10. Data Security

We apply appropriate security measures, including secure hosting, access controls, and confidentiality obligations.

11. Cookies

We may use cookies to ensure site functionality and improve user experience. You can control cookies through your browser settings.
